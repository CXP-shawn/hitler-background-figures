Arthur Neville Chamberlain was born on 18 March 1869 at a house called Southbourne in the Edgbaston district of Birmingham.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTECrozier2004%E2%80%9309-2) He was the only son of the second marriage of Joseph

 Chamberlain, who later became Mayor of Birmingham and a Cabinet minister. His mother was Florence Kenrick, a cousin of William Kenrick MP "William Kenrick (Birmingham MP)"); she died when he was a small boy. Joseph Chamberlain had had another son, Austen Chamberlain, by his first marriage.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTEMacklin200611-3) The Chamberlain family were Unitarian, though Joseph lost personal religious faith by the time Neville was six years old and never required religious adherence of his children.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-lapsed-4) Neville, who disliked attending worship services of any kind and showed no interest in organised religion, described himself as a Unitarian with no stated faith and also a "reverent agnostic".[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-lapsed-4)

Neville Chamberlain was educated at home by his elder sister Beatrice Chamberlain and later at Rugby School.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmart20102%E2%80%933-5) Joseph Chamberlain then sent Neville to Mason College,[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmart20105%E2%80%936-6) now the University of Birmingham. Neville Chamberlain had little interest in his studies there, and in 1889 his father apprenticed 

him to a firm of accountants.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTESmart20106%E2%80%938-7) Within six months he became a salaried employee.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTESelf200621-8) In an effort to recoup diminished family fortunes, Joseph Chamberlain sent his younger son to establish a sisal plantation on Andros Island in the Bahamas.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTESelf200622-9) Neville Chamberlain spent six years there but the plantation was a failure, and Joseph Chamberlain lost £50,000[\[a\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-plantation_lose-10)[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDutton20019-11) (equivalent to £5,684,000 in 2025).[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neville_Chamberlain#cite_note-inflation-UK-12)

**Arthur Neville Chamberlain** (/ˈtʃeɪmbərlɪn/; 18 March 1869–9 November 1940) was a British politician who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from May 1937 to May 1940 and Leader of the Conservative Party "Leader of the Conservative Party (UK)") from May 1937 to October 1940. He is best known for his foreign policy of appeasement, and in particular for his signing of the Munich Agreement on 30 September 1938, ceding the German-speaking Sudetenland region of Czechoslovakia to Nazi Germany led by Adolf Hitler. Following the invasion of Poland on 1 September 1939, which marked the beginning of World War II, Chamberlain announced the declaration of war on Germany "British declaration of war on Germany (1939)") two days later and led the United Kingdom through the first eight months of the war until his resignation as prime minister on 10 May 1940.

After working in business and local government, and after a short spell as [Director of National Service]

(https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Director_of_National_Service "Director of National Service") in 1916 and 1917, Chamberlain followed his father Joseph Chamberlain and elder half-brother Austen Chamberlain in becoming a Member of Parliament in the 1918 general election for the new Birmingham Ladywood#Elections_in_the_1910s "Birmingham Ladywood (UK Parliament constituency)") division at the age of 49. He declined a junior ministerial position, remaining a backbencher until 1922. He was rapidly promoted in 1923 to Minister of Health "Minister of Health (United Kingdom)") and then Chancellor of the Exchequer. After a short-lived Labour "Labour Party (UK)")-led government, he returned as Minister of Health, introducing a range of reform measures from 1924 to 1929. He was appointed Chancellor of the Exchequer in the National Government "National Government (1931–1935)") in 1931.

Chamberlain succeeded Stanley Baldwin as prime minister on 28 May 1937. His premiership was dominated by the question of policy towards an increasingly aggressive Germany, and his actions at Munich were widely popular among the British at the time. In response to Hitler's continued aggression, Chamberlain pledged the United Kingdom to defend Poland's independence if the

 latter were attacked, an alliance that brought his country into declaring war on Germany after it invaded Poland, which resulted in the "Phoney War", but not in any substantial assistance to Poland's fight against the aggression. The failure of Allied forces to prevent the German invasion of Norway caused the House of Commons to hold the Norway Debate in May 1940. Chamberlain's conduct of the war was heavily criticised by members of all parties and, in a vote of confidence, his government's majority was greatly reduced.
