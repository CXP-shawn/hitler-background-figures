## Early life

Reinhard Tristan Eugen Heydrich[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDederichs200911-8) was born on 7 March 1904 in Halle an der Saale to composer and opera singer Richard Bruno Heydrich and his wife, Elisabeth Anna Maria Amalia Heydrich (née Krantz). His father came from a Protestant family, but converted to Elisabeth's Roman Catholic faith upon marriage.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth201121-9) Although he was not of Jewish descent, rumours that Richard Heydrich had Jewish ancestry stemmed from the fact that his stepfather Robert Gustav Süss appeared to have a Jewish-sounding surname.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth201113-10)

During his youth, Heydrich was an altar boy, attending evening prayers and Mass every week with his mother as part of the Catholic minority in Halle.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth201122-11) Two of his forenames were musical references: "Reinhard" referred to the hero from his father's opera _Amen_, and "Tristan" stems from Richard Wagner's _Tristan und Isolde_. Heydrich's third name, "Eugen", was his late maternal grandfather's forename ( Eugen Krantz had been the director of the Dresden Royal Conservatory).[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth201114%E2%80%9318-12)

Heydrich's family held social standing and substantial financial means. Music was a part of Heydrich's everyday life; his father founded the Halle Conservatory of Music, Theatre, and Teaching and his mother taught piano there.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth201114,_20-13) As the oldest son, Reinhard was expected to inherit his father's music conservatory and was trained in music by his father, learning both the piano and violin when he was six years old.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth201121-9) Heydrich developed a passion for the violin and carried that interest into adulthood; he impressed listeners with his musical talent.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDederichs200928-14)

**Reinhard Tristan Eugen Heydrich** (/ˈhaɪdrɪk/; German:[\[ˈʁaɪnhaʁtˈtʁɪstanˈʔɔʏɡn̩ˈhaɪdʁɪç\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German")Play audioⓘ; 7 March 1904– 4 June 1942) was a high-ranking SS and police official in Nazi Germany as well as one of the principal architects of the Holocaust. He held the rank of SS-_Obergruppenführer und General der Polizei_. Many historians regard Heydrich as one of the most sinister figures within the Nazi regime.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTESereny1996325-5)[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEEvans200553-6)[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGerwarth2011xiii-7) Adolf Hitler described him as "the man with the iron heart."[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich#cite_note-FOOTNOTEDederichs200992-4)

Beginning in September 1939, Heydrich was chief of the Reich Security Main Office (including the Gestapo, Kripo "Kriminalpolizei (Nazi Germany)"), and SD). He was also _Stellvertretender Reichsprotektor_ (Deputy Reich-Protector) of Bohemia and Moravia. He served as president of the International Criminal Police Commission (ICPC, now known as Interpol) and chaired the January 1942 Wannsee Conference which formalised plans for the " Final Solution to the Jewish question"—the deportation and genocide of all Jews in German-occupied Europe.

Heydrich was the founding head of the _Sicherheitsdienst_ (Security Service, SD), an intelligence organisation charged with seeking out and neutralising resistance to the Nazi Party via arrests, deportations, and murders. He helped organise _Kristallnacht_, a series of coordinated attacks against Jews throughout Nazi Germany and parts of Austria on 9–10 November 1938. The attacks were carried out by SA stormtroopers and civilians and presaged the Holocaust. Upon his arrival in Prague, Heydrich sought to eliminate opposition to the Nazi occupation by suppressing Czech culture and deporting and executing members of the Czech resistance. He was directly responsible for the _Einsatzgruppen_, the special task forces that travelled in the wake of the German armies and murdered more than two million people by mass shooting and gassing including 1.3million Jews.
