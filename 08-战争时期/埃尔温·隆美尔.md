## Early life and career

Rommel was born on 15 November 1891 in Heidenheim, 45 kilometres (28mi) from Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg, then p

art of the German Empire. He was the third of five children to Erwin Rommel senior (1860–1913) and his wife Helene von Luz. Her father, Karl von Luz, headed the local government council. As a young man, Rommel's father had been an artillery lieutenant. Rommel had one older sister who was an art teacher and his favourite sibling, one older brother named Manfred who died in infancy, and two younger brothers, of whom one became a successful dentist and the other an opera singer.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FOOTNOTEFraser19938-11)

At age 18, Rommel joined the Württemberg Infantry Regiment No. 124 in Weingarten as a _Fähnrich_ ( ensign "Ensign (rank)")), in 1910, studying at the Officer Cadet School "Kriegsschule (Germany)") in Danzig.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FOOTNOTEHoffman200410-12) He graduated in November 1911 and was commissioned as a lieutenant in January 1912 and was assigned to the 124th Infantry in Weingarten.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FOOTNOTEButler201530%E2%80%9331-13) He was posted to Ulm in March 1914 to the 49th Field Artillery Regiment, XIII (Royal Württemberg) Corps_Corps "XIII (Royal Württemberg) Corps"), as a battery commander. He returned to the 124th when war was declared.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FOOTNOT

**Johannes Erwin Eugen Rommel** (pronounced[\[ˈɛɐviːnˈʁɔməl\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German")Play audioⓘ; 15 November 1891– 14 October 1944), known as **The Desert Fox** ( German: _Wüstenfuchs_, pronounced[\[ˈvyːstn̩ˌfʊks\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Standard_German "Help:IPA/Standard German")Play audioⓘ), was a German _Generalfeldmarschall_ (field marshal) during World War II. He served in the _Wehrmacht_ of Nazi Germany, as well as in the _Reichswehr_ of the Weimar Republic, and Imperial German 

Army of the German Empire.

Rommel was a highly decorated officer in World War I and awarded the _Pour le Mérite_ for his actions on the Italian Front "Italian Front (World War I)"). In 1937, he published his classic book on military tactics, _Infantry Attacks_, drawing on his experiences in that war. In World War II, he commanded the 7th Panzer Division "7th Panzer Division (Wehrmacht)") during the 1940 invasion of France. His leadership of German and Italian forces in the North African campaign established his reputation as one of the ablest tank commanders of the war, and earned him the nickname _der Wüstenfuchs_, "the Desert Fox". Among his British adversaries he had a reputation for chivalry, and his phrase "war without hate" has been uncritically used to describe the North African campaign.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-2) Other historians have rejected the phrase as a myth, citing crimes against North African Jewish populations.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-3) Others note there is no clear evidence Rommel was involved in or aware of these crimes.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGabel2014202-4)[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-5) He later commanded the German forces opposing the Allied cross-channel invasion of Normandy in June 1944.

After the Nazis gained power, Rommel pledged allegiance to the new regime. However, historians have given different accounts of the specific period and his motivations.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FrankAllg112012-6) At least until near the war's end, he was a loyal supporter of Adolf Hitler, but not to the Nazi party and SS.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-FOOTNOTESearle201426-7) In 1944, Rommel was implicated in the 20 July plot (or Operation Valkyrie) to assassinate Hitler. Subsequently, Rommel was given a choice between suicide or facing a trial that would result in his disgrace and execution. He ultimately chose the former and took a cyanide pill.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-8) Rommel was given a state funeral, and it was announced he had succumbed to injuries from the strafing of his car in Normandy. He is considered the most well-known general on any side of World War II, with his actions in the war still attracting major attention until this day.[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-9)[](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erwin_Rommel#cite_note-10)

rilliant commander and a victim of Nazi Germany, although others have contested this assessment and called it the " Rommel myth". Rommel's reputation for conducting a clean war was used in the interest of the West German rearmament and reconciliation between the former enemies – the UK and the US on one side and the new Federal Republic of Germany on the other.
